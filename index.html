What this project does

Science fiction usually shows the finished artifact: the starship, cybernetic implant, terraformed planet, autonomous factory, or synthetic person. This atlas asks what would actually need to be powered, cooled, manufactured, inspected, qualified, and scaled.

How to read the score

Reality Score is a present-day capability score, not a prediction.

Scores are interpretive engineering estimates. A high score means the closest real analogue performs a meaningful fraction of the fictional function today. A low score means the fictional system still depends on unresolved physics, extreme materials, impossible energy density, or unproven integration.

Scoring method

Reality Score = weighted engineering readiness

The numbers are deliberately approximate. They make the engineering gap visible; they are not forecasts, market predictions, or claims that a fictional system will inevitably exist.

Worked example

Directed-energy weapons

Physics is real and fielded systems exist, but sci-fi capability is paced by power delivery, beam control, thermal rejection, platform integration, and cost per useful shot. That creates high partial reality without Star Wars-level performance.

Limitations: fictional systems vary across canon, real analogues rarely map perfectly, and fast-moving fields can change. The goal is not to settle sci-fi debates; it is to expose the manufacturability, qualification, energy, reliability, and scale problems underneath the imagined artifact.
